How electricity powers our world

The energy grid is one of the most important systems in modern life. It connects power plants, transmission lines, substations, and homes so that electricity can be generated, delivered, and used safely.

Why the energy grid matters

Every time we turn on a light, charge a phone, or use a microwave, we depend on a massive electrical system working behind the scenes. The grid must balance supply and demand, move energy efficiently, and keep people safe.

A system built for everyday life

The electrical grid is not just wires and poles. It is a carefully coordinated system that helps hospitals run, businesses operate, schools function, and families stay connected.

Understanding the grid also helps people understand energy costs, conservation, renewable energy, and safety.
